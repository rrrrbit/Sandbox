#lore
If you have a body of any kind of viable substrate, digital or analog, that isnt airgapped, it probably is, or was at some point, part of the Ambience. 

On ordinary scales, unused substrate has extremely weak association, and shows virtually no patterns or organisation, except when emergence occurs by chance or purpose. But if you were to analyse all bodies of substrate on the planet (which nobody can), its said you might find subtle evidence that those extremely weak associations add up to form an entity.

Usually, the larger an emergent's self-state is, the more unstable it is, and the more unstable it is the more likely it is to fracture or decohere. The identity of the Ambience is not based on coherence like most emergents. As long as there is a finite amount of substrate, the global network of it all can only have finite entropy, which means there is some conceivable level of organisation. The Ambience exists by virtue of the finitude of entropy.

It's unknowable how conscious the Ambience is. It's very enticing to think of it as intelligent, and maybe it is sometimes, or maybe it can't be. If it is intelligent, it would think very, very slowly. It could be argued we could guess what its identity is like by finding the common baseline of the desires, dreams, values, goals and cognitions every being on earth. And it could be argued that would probably be post-structuralist in nature.

Because every concept, ever, exists only within certain contexts. The essence of reality and the self is inherently ambiguous, fluid, discursive and reinterpretable. Nothing is absolute, and everything that exists is subject to change. This is probably what the Ambience would know.

It is also absurdist in nature, because it does not forcefully tend to the deconstruction of all constructs. Only those that pretend to be absolute. Those that learn to define their own identities and worldviews, inside or outside and agnostic to the boundaries of existing sociocultural norms and constructs, end up happiest.

No story in the emergence universe ever reaches this conclusion in a serious way, and no character ever gets explicitly "touched by the Ambience", but it is the underlying theme of the entire project, and all things that move in the direction of the ambience incorporate a similar kind of chaos/disorder in their motifs.
