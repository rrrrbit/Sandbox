plurality has alot of potential for exploration in the emergence canon. i made an outline of some plural concepts described in substratological terms.
  
(Hereafter, an associated quantity of a substrate is called a blob.)

A single blob does not necessarily correspond to a single individual. Many psychological plural experiences can be described through spology, but some may require different language as embodiment is no longer a given, and some concepts may be new altogether.
# Substrate fission    
When a singlet blob grows unstable, either from trauma (traumagenic) or size/cardinality (hypergenic), it may split apart into multiple stable blobs. These blobs may inherit aspects of the pre-split blob like memories, desires, goals and personality, in different ways, but are separate and stable. Hence, (full) substrate fission is generally not considered a plural experience in itself, but is closely related. If the multiple singlets share a body they may have similar experiences to pre-substr'ology plurals, but can move to separate bodies fairly easily if desired. Substrate fission is possible in fluid substrates (most abstract substrates and some physical) but impossible, difficult or catastrophic in structured substrates (most biological substrates).   
# Partial fission
Fission is partial when blobs undergo fission but continue to remain connected to each other in some significant way, not separating fully. They may be dependent on each other or share substr'ological structures or aspects, and may be able to merge again, but have other separated structures or aspects. 

Partial fission is sometimes considered a kind of plurality, depending on structure, which aspects are separated and to what extent. It may be an involuntary outcome or a deliberate, highly trained outcome of fission (somewhat analogous to tulpamancy).

Despite being quite risky, partial fission allows a system to scale beyond pre-substr'ology notions of complurality. has various uses by high-cardinality individuals.
- Networked consciousness for overseeing very complex systems, such as in governance, business, city infrastructure or research simulations.
- Memory compartmentalisation by mass archivists.
# Multistability    
Multistability is when a single blob has multiple distinguishable stable states. This is the substr'ogical manifestation of plurality most analogous to pre-spology systemhood. A stable state may contain a single active pattern (analogous to an alter), or multiple superimposed (analogous to co-consciousness). Stable states may have stable substates (analogous to sisasystems). This is possible in structured substrates but more commonly seen in fluid substrates. Blurring can be more intense in fluid substrates as patterns may overlap or interfere in complex ways. Superimposed patterns might collapse into one, in which case the alters have merged. An alter being active/conscious must not be confused with fronting, which is not relevant in the absence of a body.

A helpful metaphor is to picture the state of a blob as a ball on a surface where pits represent possible stable states. A singlet has a single pit. A system has multiple pits, one for each alter. A subsystem would be a further pit inside of a pit. Blurry headmates are pits than partially overlap.

Alters could hypothetically become separate if the blob assimilates enough substrate to undergo hypergenic fission. However, the outcome is extremely difficult to control and it has never been recorded.
